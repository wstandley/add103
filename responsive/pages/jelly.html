Jelly Roll

Jelly Roll’s music career began with the release of his first album, The Big Sal Story, in 2012, which showcased his unique style. He continued to gain attention with his 2016 album, Sobriety Sucks, and followed it up with Addiction Kills in 2017. These early works laid the foundation for his sound, combining heartfelt lyrics with a blend of genres.

His 2020 album, Self Medicated, featured the powerful single “Save Me,” which delves into personal struggles and has resonated deeply with listeners. This track helped him gain significant recognition in the country music scene.

In 2021, Jelly Roll released Ballads of the Broken, which included the hit single “Son of a Sinner.” This song showcases his storytelling abilities and vulnerability, further solidifying his place in the music industry.

While I don’t listen to a lot of country music compared to other genres, having this personal link to Jelly Roll has easily placed him among my favorite artists. Along with him, I also enjoy listening to other country artists like Thomas Rhett, Old Dominion, Kenny Chesney, and Kane Brown. Jelly Roll’s authentic lyrics and raw style make his music memorable, and it’s exciting to see him making waves in country music.
